Improving Access to Quality Education in Rural Rwanda

Supporting students in underserved communities through practical digital learning, teacher support, and community-driven education solutions.

Explore My Journey

My Entrepreneurial Journey

This project began with a simple question: how can rural students receive the same quality learning opportunities as students in better-resourced areas?

The Spark

I focused on the education challenges faced by rural students in Rwanda, especially in Nyagatare District, where limited infrastructure and learning resources affect student success.

Research & Discovery

Through interviews, surveys, and secondary research, I explored barriers such as long travel distances, limited digital tools, teacher shortages, and financial pressure on families.

Vision Formation

The vision is to create a practical, affordable, and sustainable education support system that uses offline digital learning and community participation.

Our Solution: EduAccess Learning Centers

A community-supported digital learning initiative built for rural schools with limited internet and electricity access.

Solar-Powered Classrooms

Learning spaces powered by solar energy so students can access digital resources even where electricity is unreliable.

Offline Learning Content

Educational videos, exercises, and lessons stored offline so schools do not depend on constant internet connectivity.

Teacher Training

Teachers receive digital literacy support and training to use technology effectively in lessons.

Shared Devices

Tablets and computers are shared by students to improve access to modern learning tools.

Prototype Development

The idea developed through research, stakeholder feedback, and gradual improvement.

Stage 1: Problem Research

Identified the core problem of limited access to quality education in rural Rwanda, focusing on Nyagatare District.

Stage 2: Stakeholder Interviews

Collected insights from a teacher, parent, and student to understand the real-life barriers affecting education.

Stage 3: Solution Design

Designed a solution combining solar power, offline content, shared devices, and teacher support.

Stage 4: Lean Canvas

Mapped the problem, solution, customer segments, channels, costs, revenue streams, and key success metrics.

Reflection & Learning

The research showed that rural education challenges are deeply connected and require practical, community-centered solutions.

Challenges Faced

  • Limited learning materials
  • Long travel distances
  • Low access to internet and electricity
  • Financial pressure on families
  • Teachers needing more training and support

Lessons Learned

  • Students and teachers are motivated, but they need better tools.
  • Offline solutions are important in low-connectivity areas.
  • Community involvement improves adoption and sustainability.
  • Teacher training is just as important as student access.

User Feedback

Stakeholders showed interest in local, affordable solutions that fit rural realities instead of relying only on expensive internet-based platforms.

Future Vision & Plans

The next stage is to test the solution in a small pilot before expanding to more rural schools.

Short-Term Plan

  • Build a low-fidelity prototype
  • Collect user feedback from students and teachers
  • Refine the learning center design

Medium-Term Plan

  • Pilot in selected rural schools
  • Train teachers on digital learning tools
  • Partner with NGOs and district education offices

Long-Term Plan

  • Scale to more rural districts
  • Improve content in local languages
  • Create a sustainable funding model through grants, sponsorships, and partnerships
